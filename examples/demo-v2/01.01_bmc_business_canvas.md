# Business Model Canvas (01.01_bmc_business_canvas.md)

> [!TIP]
> The Business Model Canvas (BMC) is a strategic management tool that allows developing and sketching new or existing business models.

## 1. Problem
> What is the main problem, pain point, or inefficiency we are trying to solve?
- Difficulty in creating consistent architectural documentation and proactively gathering requirements or edge cases that frequently go unnoticed.
- The lack of flexibility to discover blind spots and adjust decisions in real-time during project discovery.
- Overly simple prompts that generate technical inaccuracy when directing Artificial Intelligence models for coding, resulting in failures due to superficial specifications.

## 2. Value Proposition
> What makes this solution unique and valuable to the user?
- Differentiated ability to generate and integrate multiple documentation methodologies into a single continuous flow.
- Acts as an educational guide that makes the user think and understand the architecture, escaping the blind reliance on 100% automated solutions ("vibe coding").
- Directs the AI for coding with much higher precision than isolated prompts, facilitating the progressive understanding of software engineering in practice.

## 3. Customer Segments
> Who exactly will experience this pain and use the system?
- Development professionals of all seniority levels (from beginners to advanced engineers and architects), where the tool's flexibility adapts to the project's complexity and the technical level of the provided answers.
- Profiles focused on business or product (like Product Managers) who wish to structure requirements and ensure solid documentation before the software engineering team gets involved.
- In other words, any profile, from beginners to advanced. The interview molds itself to the quality level of the answers and the project's complexity.

## 4. Viability and Sustainability
> How does this project generate value for the ecosystem or save resources (time/money)?
- Upfront planning and robust documentation prevent wasting time with errors and heavy rework during development.
- Identifying scope flaws earlier reduces the back-and-forth of unnecessary corrections, accelerating the final delivery (*time-to-market*).
- Optimizes the use of AIs and saves *tokens*, ensuring that the code generated on the very first try is much more accurate and aligned with the goal.

## 5. Key Partnerships
> Which external partners, providers, or key frameworks are essential for it to work?
- Compatible environments and tools: CLIs, IDEs, and Artificial Intelligence Model providers.

## 6. Cost Structure
> Main costs inherent to operation and development. (Note: Without estimating precise numerical values).
- Open-source project maintained as a hobby; there is no rigid financial control, depending only on personal time and occasional basic AI token costs.

## 7. Revenue Streams / Gains
> Ways in which the project monetizes or generates indirect gains.
- None. The project is solely focused on technical learning and public utility, with no commercial gains foreseen.
